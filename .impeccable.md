# IKKON — Design Context

## Design Context

### Game Summary
**一刀入魂 IKKON** is a group-buy gacha raffle. Players pick a "Disaster Beast" pool, choose a Blademaster character at a rarity tier (Common ¥99 → Legend ¥129), and when the pool fills, one winner takes the grand prize (Switch 2, PS5, etc.). Every participant gets a guaranteed smaller drop. Unwanted drops melt into Soul Coins. 30 characters across 5 classes, each with a looping character video. Mobile-first, max-width 480px, Traditional Chinese.

---

### Users
General Taiwanese consumers aged 18–35 who are curious about ichiban-kuji (一番賞) style collectible raffles. They don't need to be hardcore gamers or anime fans — a regular person should see this and think "wow, a raffle can feel this premium and immersive." The experience should feel approachable and exciting, not intimidating or niche.

---

### Brand Personality
**Magical · Ceremonial · Warm**

The brand should feel like cracking open a wizarding world collectibles shop — part Harry Potter, part Japanese capsule gacha, part premium unboxing experience. There's genuine wonder in the moment of entering and revealing. Not dark and edgy; magical and alive.

---

### Aesthetic Direction

**Direction: Fantasy Magic — Warm Midnight**

Think: candlelit Great Hall, enchanted manuscript, magical artefact shop. Deep midnight blues and inky purples as the base — but warmed by amber, parchment gold, and candlelight, never pure black. Stars, particles, subtle shimmer. Aged-paper textures over cold noise textures.

**Palette shift:**
- Background: Deep midnight blue-purple (#0d0d2b → #1a0a2e) not pure black
- Primary accent: Warm amber-gold (#f0b429) — like enchanted candles
- Secondary accent: Soft violet-blue (#7c6df0) — spell-cast glow
- Surface: Parchment-tinted dark (#1c1635) with subtle paper texture
- Text: Warm cream (#f5f0e8) not cold white

**Typography:**
- Display/titles: More ornate serif — cinzel, cormorant garamond, or similar magical-feeling font
- Chinese titles: Keep Noto Serif TC but use it more elegantly
- HUD numbers: Can stay Bebas Neue for price/count displays
- More generous sizing; less cramped

**Radius & Shape:**
- Slightly softer than current (8–12px max) — aged book corners, not pure blade-edge
- Cards feel like physical collectible cards or spell book pages
- Buttons feel like wax-sealed decisions, not UI widgets

**Textures & Effects:**
- Aged parchment/vellum texture on surfaces
- Particle effects: floating stars, magical sparkles, spell dust
- Reveal moment: magical explosion of light and particles
- Beast cards: illustrated like bestiary pages, not game HUD slots

**What it should NOT look like:**
- Pure black fighting game HUD (current direction — abandon this)
- Cold dark mobile game UI
- Emoji standing in for character art
- Generic purple gradient fantasy (cheap wizard aesthetic)
- Overcrowded, information-dense screens

---

### Design Principles

1. **Characters are the hero** — Real character art/video front and center. Never emojis. The moment you see a character, you should feel the IP.

2. **Magical warmth, not cold dark** — The palette lives in deep midnight blue/purple but always has amber/gold warmth. Think candlelight in a dark library, not a black void.

3. **Ceremony over UI** — Every key action (joining, revealing, winning) should feel like a magical ritual, not a button press. Animation and transition are part of the product.

4. **Legible to anyone** — A 45-year-old who's never played a gacha game should understand the flow in 30 seconds. Clear hierarchy, accessible copy, no jargon walls.

5. **Premium collectible energy** — This should feel like holding a physical limited-edition item. Tactile textures, weight in animations, satisfaction in interaction.
